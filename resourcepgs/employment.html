EMPLOYMENT

There comes a time when you ought to start doing what you want. Take a job that you love. You will jump out of bed in the morning. I think you are out of your mind if you keep taking jobs that you don't like because you think it will look good on your resume. Isn't that a little like saving up sex for your old age?

Pro Tip: Everyone suffers from Imposter Syndrome. If embarking on a new career, you will feel stress. You will not immediately understand what's going on as you get up-to-speed. This does not mean you are inadequate, it just means that you are learning.


Job Searching

It's been a while since you've searched for a job, and rejection sucks. This can get painful. Listen, Epic's HR practices are well known and well respected by companies across many industries, but that doesn't mean that this will be - or should be - a cake walk. If you believe such, then understand that it's exactly this thinking that will make landing something you love burdensome. Having come from a company widely known for only taking the best and brightest low-single-digit percentage of applicants is a mark in your favor, not a golden key.

If you want something that makes you want to get out of bed in the morning, then know that finding a job must become a job... you'll need to put work into it.


  • Create a job search/professional-looking email address
    • This goes on your resume, online applications, LinkedIn, etc. and will be how people know you before meeting you
    • FirstName.mInitial.LastName@email.com is simple and respectable
    • Your own domain is more costly, but an option for more common names
    • No one wants to see or hire the email address you created in highschool
  • Most worthwhile job search sites offer advanced options to target your search
    • City, State, ZIP, and Radius (x miles of) filters
    • Keywords, both to include and exclude
    • Job Function and Type of Job (full time, internship, etc.)
    • Education and Experience
    • Industry
    • Salary
  • Search all the things!
  • Monster.com
  • CareerBuilder.com
  • Google for Jobs
  • Indeed.com
  • Glassdoor.com
  • LinkedIn.com
  • Dice.com
  • Idealist.com
  • TheLadders.com
  • LinkUp.com
  • ZipRecruiter.com
  • US.jobs
  • Investigate the company and the role as you apply, and recognize that you might need to customize your resume to match the position
  • Rejection can be hard, but you need to not take it personally... sometimes they already knew who they were going to hire, but had to go through the process of posting the job
  • You won't even hear back from some jobs (like when I applied to be Global Product Lead for the Halo franchize, or my friend applied to be the UW-Madison Football Head Coach)
  • Don't stop applying to jobs even if you found your dream job
