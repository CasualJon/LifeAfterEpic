Life After

Background

Years ago, just before my interview, I was told Epic is a great place to start a career.
I did not fully understood what that meant at the time, but after more than a decade of Staff Meeting, I began to see the message in those words held multiple truths.

This website does not exist out of malice or spite, it is not here with the intention of fueling or validating any emotion, nor will it attempt to justify any part of your experience from any side. Few depart Epic on such sour terms that look back only to find bad memories and continuing frustration. Some were unfairly given the short end of the stick, some were never given an honest chance. While I've seen it enough to understand, for that I believe someone out there has a blog you can reference.

Like many companies, Epic does some things right and other things wrong. Very likely, you were working enough hard hours that much of your social circle was tied to a core group of coworkers. Your family often asked about work and how it's going. It's no wonder that leaving has the familiar feeling of ending a relationship.

Thus, here you will find things that, I hope, will be of help to you regardless of whether you're thinking about leaving or your time on campus is long since past. If you have suggestions or questions, get in touch.

Jon
