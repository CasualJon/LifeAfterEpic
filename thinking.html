You're going to do great things!

Before you start that journey, below are things to consider as you make your plan.


  • Health insurance
    Your health insurance remains active for the calendar month of your departure.
    If possible, consider an end date earlier in the month and get those doctor/dental
    appointments on the books. If you do not have another option for health insurance
    (spouse/partner, another job), you are able to use COBRA to extend your existing coverage.

  • 401(k)
    You can leave your 401(k) with Fidelity if you like what you have setup.
    To roll your 401(k) over into an IRA, you'll need an open Individual Retirement Account.
    You'll call Fidelity, they will ask for the institution holding your IRA, then mail YOU
    a check for your balance (EOD of the day you call) to liquidate the account.
    You will then need to send the check to your IRA institution.
    Read about your options.

  • Stock Appreciation Rights
    If you have SARs, you will need to request payout.
    Depending on the amount, you may need to make a early tax payment to the IRS.
    There are additional conditions, but if you expect to owe at least $1,000 in
    federal tax for the year (after subtracting withholdings and credits), the IRS may
    require a quarterly estimated tax payment.     If unsure, consult a professional.

  • Eureka
    You'll need access to Eureka (UtiliPro) after your departure for things like W2s, past
    pay stubs, contact details for benefits, etc. Update your Eureka password before your
    departure (to reset the change required/lockout counter), and save the link on your home
    computer so you'll remember it.

  • NVTs
    Not everyone intends head toward consulting in their post-Epic career. Even if you
    don't, make the time to update your certifications. It won't hurt to have a fallback,
    and you are unable to do update any certifications during the non-compete period.

  • Clean your PC/laptop
    Much of your life probably lives on your work machine. PDFs from a mortgage,
    account informaiton (websites/rewards programs), music, resum√©s, pictures, those
    rough drafts of your wedding vows... You need not worry about inappropriate use
    (CaTS is solid), but you will want or need these things for yourself.

  • Update account contact info
    You may have many accounts where your listed contact information includes your Epic
    email address, phone number, or address. Upadte these with your personal contact
    details before your departure. Resetting passwords or making changes later difficult.

  • TLG data
    All that time you logged in DeLorean/TimeTurner/Text. Some professional credentials
    and certifications (or even future employers) will want details about the number of
    hours spent on certain types of tasks or domains.

  • Recognize that not everyone will know how to respond
    Some won't understand, some will be jealous, some will think of how it affects them.
    That's not for you to worry over, you don't need the approval of others.
    Some will be sad to see you go. Some will be excited for you.
    Thank these people for the time you were able to share together.
    Most will ask you what you're going to do next, but that doesn't mean you need to know;
    your don't have to let a career define you.

  • Don't be a jerk
    Pull your weight until the exit interview.

Still doing good, working hard, having fun, making money.
